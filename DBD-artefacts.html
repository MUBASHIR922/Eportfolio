Big Data and Database Systems - Artefact Summary

Unit 1

In Unit 1, I was introduced to the module and engaged in discussions on how organisations leverage big data, reflecting on differences between traditional databases and emerging technologies. This helped me set personal learning goals, such as improving my database design skills.

Unit 2 - 3

Unit 2 deepened my understanding of core relational database principles like modelling and normalisation. Studying how proper schema design reduces data redundancy and improves integrity gave me a strong theoretical foundation and boosted my confidence for upcoming practical tasks.
Unit 3 marked the start of our team project. I assumed the role of Database Designer, contributing to scoping a retail data integration scenario and drafting an initial ER schema for the project. This contribution linked theory to practice and provided the blueprint for development.

Unit 4 - 5

Unit 4 involved intensive database design and dataset mapping. I led the creation of the ER diagram, normalising the schema to 3NF. Translating abstract design principles into a concrete schema (tables, keys, relationships) was pivotal in my development, and I coordinated with our Data Manager to align the model with the actual dataset.
During Unit 5, we tackled data cleaning and pipeline planning. I adjusted the schema based on quirks in the real dataset, learning to be flexible with design. At the same time, I became aware of data governance and ethics as our team addressed GDPR compliance and data quality issues. I ensured our evolving ETL plan reflected these considerations, underscoring the need for iterative design and regulatory compliance.

Unit 6

Unit 6 was about integrating our work and submitting the group report. I validated the final schema and ER diagram while helping polish the report with structural edits. Our team lead acknowledged my swift review, reflecting strong collaboration. Tutor feedback was very positive (distinction-level) and suggested adding discussion of challenges like cloud integration. I took this on board, and the distinction boosted my confidence..

Unit 7 - 9

Unit 7 introduced big data frameworks and NoSQL paradigms. I did not actively participate – a missed opportunity to discuss scaling and non-relational databases – but reviewing the material later (e.g. ACID vs CAP theorem trade-offs) reinforced why our project’s relational approach was appropriate.

Unit 8 focused on the Internet of Things (IoT) and its data implications. I contributed a discussion post evaluating IoT’s benefits for data science and raised concerns about security, privacy, and data governance. Peers found my analysis insightful, but their feedback highlighted aspects I missed (e.g. environmental impact and interoperability challenges). This taught me to broaden my perspective on technology solutions.

In Unit 9, meant for peer reviews, I did not fully engage in giving feedback. However, I received useful critiques on my IoT post, for instance, a peer urged me to propose solutions for IoT security issues rather than focusing only on risks. I learned to balance critical analysis with solution-oriented thinking, an insight I carried into the final project.

Unit 10 - 11

In Unit 10, I helped transform our design into an implemented system. I refined the ERD and created the schema in PostgreSQL, enforcing integrity constraints and adding indexes for performance. I also co-designed the ETL pipeline to extract, transform, and load data into the unified database, applying cleaning rules to ensure consistency. Seeing these components come together solidified my technical skills, as theory from earlier units became a working reality.

In Unit 11, we focused on testing and final analysis. Using sample data, we verified that the ETL pipeline correctly cleaned and merged records, and that the database supported accurate reporting. I ran queries to confirm that meaningful insights (e.g. sales trends) could be derived, and we outlined a plan for a real-time dashboard to visualise these results. We delivered a single source of truth.

Unit 12

Unit 12 was a consolidation and reflection phase. I reviewed the entire journey and drafted a comprehensive 1000-word reflection (submitted separately) analysing my technical and professional growth. This final step allowed me to link each unit’s experiences to my development as a database professional, highlighting how the module’s challenges and achievements have prepared me for future work in big data and database systems.
