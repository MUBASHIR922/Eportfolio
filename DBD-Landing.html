Deciphering Big Data

This module examines the key challenges of big data by exploring a range of data sources, formats, and acquisition approaches. It focuses on converting raw datasets into structured, analysis-ready outputs through essential techniques such as data cleaning and normalisation. Through practical data wrangling and evaluation methods, the module strengthens the ability to assess data quality, reliability, and organisational value, supporting effective decision-making in real-world data science contexts.

Learning Outcomes

I will learn:

  • Build a critical awareness of different data types, formats, and sourcing methods, and how these factors affect dataset reliability, integrity, and suitability for analysis.
  • To strengthen data wrangling abilities through cleaning, standardisation, and verification techniques, converting raw datasets into consistent, trustworthy, and analysis-ready formats.
  • Use exploratory data methods to examine, interpret, and communicate insights clearly, ensuring outputs remain accurate, easy to understand, and valuable over time.
  • To develop understanding of database modelling and design principles, and how they support efficient data storage, management, and analytical performance.
  • To critically assess the key challenges and risks of big data, including constraints, security vulnerabilities, and its potential to drive innovation.
  • To enhance the ability to bridge theory and practice, by applying appropriate tools and programming methods to preprocess, refine, and optimise large-scale data for practical use.
