Machine Learning - Artefact Summary

Unit 1 - Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0

The module began by exploring the evolution from Industry 4.0 to Industry 5.0 and their impact on modern organisations. I examined how automation, artificial intelligence and interconnected systems improve operational efficiency while increasing organisational dependence on digital infrastructure. Using the CrowdStrike global outage as a case study, I critically analysed how failures within interconnected systems can disrupt multiple industries. This activity strengthened my understanding of resilience, cybersecurity and governance as essential components of successful digital transformation rather than viewing technology solely as an efficiency tool.

Unit 2 - Peer Discussion and Critical Evaluation

During this unit I critically reviewed my peers' discussions on the NotPetya cyberattack against Maersk and the WannaCry attack on the NHS. My responses highlighted that many cyber incidents result from weaknesses in governance and organisational preparedness rather than technology itself. This activity developed my academic writing, critical evaluation and evidence-based argumentation skills while encouraging me to consider multiple perspectives before reaching conclusions.

Unit 3 - Reflection on Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0

This unit allowed me to consolidate my learning by reflecting on the discussions from the previous units. I recognised that Industry 5.0 complements rather than replaces Industry 4.0 by emphasising human-centred innovation, sustainability and organisational resilience. The reflection enhanced my appreciation of cybersecurity governance, ethical decision-making and business continuity as critical factors within digital transformation.

Unit 4 - Team Collaboration

Throughout this unit I maintained regular communication with my project team to plan and coordinate the collaborative machine learning project. These discussions enabled us to allocate responsibilities, clarify project objectives and prepare for the practical implementation completed later in the module. Working collaboratively strengthened my communication, planning and teamwork skills within a virtual development environment.

Unit 5 - Data Mining and Jaccard Similarity

This practical exercise introduced similarity and dissimilarity measures used in data mining and clustering. I manually calculated Jaccard similarity and Jaccard distance for multiple observations, allowing me to understand how similarity metrics identify relationships between binary datasets. Completing these calculations without relying solely on software improved my mathematical understanding of clustering techniques and their application within machine learning.

Unit 6 - Collaborative Machine Learning Project

During this unit my team successfully completed and submitted our machine learning project using the Airbnb dataset. The project involved data preparation, exploratory analysis, predictive modelling and evaluation using Python. Contributing to the project improved my practical programming skills while demonstrating how machine learning techniques can solve real-world business problems through collaborative development.

Unit 7 - ePortfolio Practical Activity

This unit focused on developing my professional ePortfolio by documenting programming activities and practical exercises. I completed the required Python implementation and reflected upon the learning process, strengthening both my technical documentation skills and my ability to communicate analytical work to a professional audience.

Unit 8 - Generative AI and the Future of Writing

I critically evaluated the opportunities and challenges presented by Generative AI within professional and academic environments. My discussion explored how AI writing systems improve productivity through report generation, summarisation and documentation while also considering risks including hallucinations, misinformation and overreliance on automated outputs. This activity reinforced the importance of ethical AI adoption, human oversight and responsible governance.

Unit 9 - Peer Review of Generative AI Discussions

I provided constructive feedback to peers discussing the ethical implications of large language models. My responses emphasised that AI should complement rather than replace professional judgement and highlighted the importance of organisational governance, validation procedures and cybersecurity. This activity further strengthened my critical thinking, academic communication and collaborative learning skills.

Unit 10 - Reflection on Generative AI

This summary reflection enabled me to consolidate my understanding of Generative AI. I concluded that although AI systems significantly improve efficiency, their successful adoption depends upon effective governance, transparency and critical human evaluation. This activity reinforced my appreciation that responsible AI implementation requires both technical capability and ethical accountability.

Unit 11 - Machine Learning Project Presentation

The final technical assessment consisted of developing and presenting a recommender system using the MovieLens 100K dataset. Our project compared a classical machine learning approach based on Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) with a Deep Learning Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF) model. I contributed to the implementation, evaluation and interpretation of both approaches using Python, NumPy, PyTorch and Scikit-learn.

The project included data preprocessing, feature engineering, model training, cross-validation, evaluation using RMSE, MAE and NDCG, explainability through SHAP and LIME, fairness analysis and deployment considerations. Presenting the project enhanced my confidence in explaining complex machine learning concepts while demonstrating how predictive models are evaluated within real-world recommender systems.

Unit 12 - Final Reflection

The final reflection provided an opportunity to evaluate my overall learning throughout the module using Rolfe et al.'s reflective framework. Reflecting upon the discussions, peer reviews, practical programming exercises and collaborative project enabled me to recognise significant growth in my technical knowledge, analytical thinking, teamwork, communication and ethical awareness. The module strengthened my understanding that successful machine learning solutions require not only accurate predictive models but also responsible governance, data quality, cybersecurity, fairness and professional judgement. These experiences have prepared me for future academic study and a professional career in Data Analytics and Machine Learning.
