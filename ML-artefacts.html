Unit 1 - Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0

Learning Activity

The first discussion explored the transition from Industry 4.0 towards Industry 5.0 and its impact on organisations. I investigated how automation, Artificial Intelligence, cloud computing and interconnected systems improve organisational efficiency whilst simultaneously increasing dependence upon digital infrastructure. I used the CrowdStrike global outage (July 2024) as a real-world case study to evaluate resilience, cybersecurity and governance.

Discussion Extract

The emergence of Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0 has significantly transformed the data analytics and business intelligence sector, an area in which I am highly interested and actively developing my skills. Industry 4.0 focuses on automation, cloud computing, artificial intelligence and interconnected systems, whilst Industry 5.0 extends this by emphasising human-centred innovation, sustainability and collaboration between humans and intelligent machines.

In my experience working with dashboards, APIs and automated reporting systems, these technologies improve decision-making speed and operational efficiency. However, they also increase organisational dependence on digital infrastructure.

A major incident demonstrating this risk was the CrowdStrike outage in July 2024. A faulty software update caused millions of Windows systems worldwide to crash, affecting airlines, banks, healthcare providers and businesses. This incident demonstrated how failures within interconnected systems can rapidly disrupt multiple industries and highlighted the importance of resilience, governance and human oversight.

Unit 5 - Jaccard Similarity and Dissimilarity

Learning Activity

This practical exercise introduced similarity measures used within Machine Learning and Data Mining. I manually calculated the Jaccard Similarity Coefficient and Jaccard Distance for several observations to understand how binary attributes are compared during clustering and pattern recognition.

Method

Gender was excluded because it is a symmetric attribute.

The remaining attributes were converted as follows:

  • Y = 1
  • P = 1
  • N = 0
  • A = 0

Jaccard Similarity

J = f11 / (f11 + f10 + f01)

Jaccard Distance

Distance = 1 − J

Dataset Used

Name Fever Cough Test-1 Test-2 Test-3 Test-4
Jack 1 0 1 0 0 0
Mary 1 0 1 0 1 0
Jim 1 1 0 0 0 0

Manual Calculations

Jack vs Mary

  • f11 = 2
  • f10 = 0
  • f01 = 1

J = 2 / (2 + 0 + 1) = 0.67

Distance = 1 − 0.67 = 0.33

Jack vs Jim

  • f11 = 1
  • f10 = 1
  • f01 = 1

J = 1 / (1 + 1 + 1) = 0.33

Distance = 1 − 0.33 = 0.67

Jim vs Mary

  • f11 = 1
  • f10 = 1
  • f01 = 2

J = 1 / (1 + 1 + 2) = 0.25

Distance = 1 − 0.25 = 0.75

Final Results

Pair Similarity Distance
Jack & Mary 0.67 0.33
Jack & Jim 0.33 0.67
Jim & Mary 0.25 0.75

Reflection

Completing these calculations manually improved my understanding of similarity measures used in clustering algorithms. Rather than relying entirely upon software, I developed a stronger appreciation of the mathematical concepts underpinning machine learning and data mining techniques.

Unit 8 - Generative AI and the Future of Writing

Learning Activity

This discussion explored the growing role of Generative Artificial Intelligence within professional and academic environments. I critically evaluated both the opportunities and limitations of AI writing systems while considering their impact on productivity, creativity, governance and ethical responsibility.

Discussion Extract

Artificial intelligence (AI) writing systems are transforming how organisations create and manage written content. From my perspective as someone developing skills in Data Analytics and Business Intelligence, AI writers should be viewed as productivity tools that support rather than replace human expertise.

AI can automate routine tasks such as report writing, summarising information and preparing documentation, allowing professionals to focus more on analysing data and making informed business decisions. However, AI-generated content may contain hallucinations, factual inaccuracies and bias if outputs are accepted without independent verification.

Whilst AI can assist creativity through idea generation and language refinement, genuine creativity remains dependent upon human experience, judgement and context. Therefore, AI should complement rather than replace human expertise.

Key Topics Covered

Unit 11 - Machine Learning Project Presentation

Learning Activity

The project compared a Classical Machine Learning algorithm (Singular Value Decomposition - SVD) with a Deep Learning model (Neural Collaborative Filtering - NCF). The objective was to determine which model produced the best recommendation performance while considering explainability, fairness and deployment.

Project Objectives

  • Develop a movie recommender system.
  • Compare Classical Machine Learning and Deep Learning.
  • Evaluate models using RMSE, MAE and NDCG.
  • Investigate Explainable AI using SHAP and LIME.
  • Evaluate fairness and deployment considerations.
  • Present findings professionally.

Technologies Used
